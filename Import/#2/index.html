History

A lot of people are curious of where exactly the logo design for NorthFace came from and what it stands for. It actually is based off the face of a mountain. More particularly from the "Half Dome" in Yosemite National Park. Orginally designed by David Alcorn in 1971.

Expanding Operations

Orginally NorthFace only made clothes and equipment for outdoor activites such as hiking and camping. But in 1981 NorthFace decided to adapt there clothing into winter wear for athletes, to better open the market for themselves and establish a more reputable name.

Staying True to the Name

Due to the company staying true to their fundamentals and delivering a high quality product thats sure to last. They've adapted over the years and have adjusted there products accordingly to please consumers. Making them more of a fashion statement while still remaining a true ever-lasting product.
